Cookies Policy

How Tool Tap uses cookies and similar technologies to improve your experience.

Effective date: January 1, 2025

1. What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by websites you visit. They help the site remember information about your visit and improve your experience.

2. Why Tool Tap uses cookies

Tool Tap uses cookies and similar technologies for the following purposes:

  • Essential functionality: To enable core site features (for example, keeping you on the correct tool page or preserving UI preferences).
  • Performance & analytics: To aggregate anonymous usage statistics so we can improve tools and fix bugs faster.
  • Preferences: To remember UI choices like theme, language, or tool settings on your next visit.

3. Types of cookies we use

  • Strictly necessary cookies — Required for site operation and cannot be disabled in our system.
  • Performance cookies — Collect anonymous usage data to help us improve speed and reliability.
  • Functional cookies — Store user preferences to provide a smoother experience.

4. Third-party cookies

We may use trusted third-party services (for example, analytics providers) that set cookies to help measure site usage and performance. These third parties have their own privacy policies and controls.

5. How to manage cookies

You can control or delete cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View cookies stored on your device.
  • Delete specific cookies or all cookies.
  • Block or restrict cookies from third parties.

Note: Blocking or deleting cookies may affect site functionality and some tools may not work correctly.

6. Data retention

Cookies are retained for different durations depending on their purpose (session vs. persistent cookies). We aim to keep cookie usage minimal and privacy-friendly.

7. Children

Tool Tap is not intended for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children.

Quick summary: Tool Tap uses minimal cookies focused on functionality and analytics. Most processing happens locally in your browser — nothing sensitive is shared unless you explicitly use a third-party integration.

8. Changes to this Cookies Policy

We may update this Cookies Policy periodically. The page's effective date will be updated when changes are made. Please check back to stay informed.
